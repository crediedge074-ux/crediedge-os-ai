# CrediEdge Dashboard Philosophy

## Purpose

The dashboard exists to answer four questions within the first 30 seconds.

1. What happened?
2. What needs my attention?
3. How healthy is my business?
4. What should I do next?

The dashboard should reduce decisions, not create them.

---

# Information Hierarchy

## 1. Morning Briefing

The first thing every user sees.

Free users receive:

- Today's Briefing

Premium users receive:

- Since You Were Last Here

The premium briefing uses AI to summarise business activity and recommend priorities.

---

## 2. Priority Actions

Three sections only.

- Messages
- Calls
- Leads

Each section displays the three highest-priority items.

The dashboard never attempts to display everything.

It surfaces only what deserves attention.

---

## 3. Business Health

Business Health becomes the heartbeat of CrediEdge OS.

It is a live score calculated from multiple business indicators including:

- Revenue
- Cash Flow
- Customer Activity
- Response Times
- Outstanding Tasks
- Reviews
- Pipeline Health
- Credit Health

The score should always explain WHY it changed.

The AI should explain HOW to improve it.

---

## 4. Business Snapshot

A visual overview of the business.

Examples include:

- Revenue
- Pipeline
- Cash Flow
- Tasks
- Customer Growth
- Reviews

These are informative rather than actionable.

---

## 5. Supporting Widgets

Additional widgets appear lower on the page.

Examples:

- Calendar
- Recent Activity
- Documents
- Automations
- Website
- Marketing
- AI Insights

These provide context but should never distract from the primary workflow.

---

# Design Principles

The dashboard should feel calm.

It should never overwhelm the user.

Every section should have a clear purpose.

Visual hierarchy is more important than the number of widgets.

Users should immediately understand where to focus without thinking.

The dashboard should feel less like traditional business software and more like the command centre for their business.
